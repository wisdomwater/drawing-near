# Drawing Near

*A 50-Day Contemplative Journey Through Leviticus*

## About

**Drawing Near** is a contemplative Christian devotional that explores the spiritual heart of the Book of Leviticus.

While Leviticus is often approached as a collection of ancient laws and temple regulations, this project seeks to read the book through the larger story of Scripture. Rather than focusing on legal or historical questions alone, these reflections explore the symbols, patterns, and imagery that reveal God's enduring desire to dwell with His people.

Each devotional follows a simple movement:

**Leviticus → Christ → The Reader's Life**

The goal is not to produce a verse-by-verse commentary, but to invite readers into prayerful reflection on the God who continually draws near.

## The Central Theme

One conviction shapes every page of this book:

> **Leviticus is ultimately about God's desire to dwell among His people.**

The sacrifices, priesthood, festivals, Sabbath laws, tabernacle, and covenant all become expressions of that single divine invitation.

Throughout the book, the reader follows the unfolding movement of Scripture:

**Garden → Tabernacle → Temple → Christ → Church → New Creation**

Seen through this lens, Leviticus becomes not merely a record of ancient worship, but another chapter in God's story of restoring communion with humanity.

## Writing Philosophy

The tone of this project is intentionally contemplative rather than argumentative.

It has been influenced by voices such as:

* Julian of Norwich
* Isaac of Nineveh
* Brother Lawrence
* George MacDonald
* Thomas Merton
* *The Cloud of Unknowing*

The reflections emphasize:

* God's faithful presence
* Mercy over fear
* Transformation through love
* Communion rather than mere obligation
* The slow work of grace
* Hope rooted in Christ

The intention is not to flatten Leviticus into allegory or reduce every passage to prediction. Instead, each chapter asks:

> **What does this reveal about the heart of God?**

## Intended Audience

This book is written for readers who:

* Have struggled to understand Leviticus
* Enjoy contemplative or devotional reading
* Desire a Christ-centered approach to the Old Testament
* Long to experience Scripture as an invitation into deeper communion with God

No prior theological training is assumed.

## Repository Purpose

This repository serves as the source for the manuscript and publishing materials for **Drawing Near**.

It preserves the text in version control, making it easier to edit, revise, and prepare future editions while documenting the development of the project.

## Closing Thought

If there is one hope behind this work, it is that readers will finish Leviticus with a renewed vision of God.

Not as a distant lawgiver demanding perfection.

But as the faithful God who has always been drawing near.

May these reflections encourage you to recognize His presence, trust His goodness, and discover that the invitation spoken in the wilderness still echoes today:

> *"I will walk among you, and will be your God, and you will be my people."* (Leviticus 26:12)
