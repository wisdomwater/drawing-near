## Day 46 — The Joy of Giving

**Leviticus 27:1–8**

**2 Corinthians 9:7**

> *"Love is measured not by the size of the gift, but by the willingness of the heart that offers it."*

Leviticus concludes with instructions for vows freely offered to God. What is striking is not simply that gifts are brought, but that provision is made for every person, regardless of wealth. If someone could not afford the assigned value, the priest would determine an offering according to that person's ability. Even here, near the end of a book often remembered for its laws, we glimpse the tenderness of God. The invitation to draw near is never reserved for those who have much. The path of worship remains open to rich and poor alike because God's desire is not to burden His people but to welcome them.

This reveals something beautiful about the heart of true giving. God has never needed our possessions. The One who fashioned the mountains and filled the seas is not enriched by silver or grain. What He desires is something far more precious: a heart that delights to respond to His love. Every offering becomes an act of trust, a quiet declaration that our lives are held within His generous care. The value of the gift has always been secondary to the love with which it is given.

Paul echoes this same truth when he writes, *"God loves a cheerful giver."* Cheerfulness is more than outward enthusiasm. It is the quiet freedom of a heart that has ceased counting the cost because it has already discovered the abundance of God's grace. Giving born from obligation leaves the soul weary, but giving born from gratitude becomes a source of joy. Love naturally longs to share what it has received. Just as a spring overflows because it is continually filled, so the heart touched by God's generosity begins to overflow toward others.

This kind of giving reaches far beyond money. We offer our attention to someone who feels unseen. We offer forgiveness where resentment once took root. We offer patience to a struggling friend, encouragement to a weary soul, hospitality to a stranger, kindness to those who cannot repay us. Sometimes the greatest gift we bring is simply our faithful presence. These quiet offerings often pass unnoticed by the world, yet they are precious in the kingdom of God, where love is the true measure of every gift.

There is also great freedom in remembering that God receives each offering according to the life we have been given. We need not compare ourselves with anyone else. Some are called to give much, while others can offer only a little. Yet in God's hands, both become holy when they arise from love. The widow's small coins became more beautiful than great treasures because they revealed a heart resting in trust. God is never keeping score; He is always cultivating communion. The offering He cherishes most is the willing heart that says, "Everything I am is Yours."

From the vows offered in Israel, to the widow's humble gift, to Christ who freely gave Himself for the life of the world, and finally to the New Creation where every act of love is gathered into God's eternal joy, Scripture tells one continuous story of generous grace. We were created in the image of the Giver, and our hearts become most fully alive when they reflect His generosity. As we receive His gifts with gratitude and share them with open hands, we discover that giving is not the loss of something we possess, but the flowering of the love that already dwells within us.

> *Today I will give with a joyful heart, trusting that love is the greatest gift I can offer.*
