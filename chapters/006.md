## Day 6 — Peace at the Table

**Leviticus 3:1–5**

**Luke 24:28–35**

> *"Peace is more than the end of conflict; it is the joy of sharing the table with God."*

Among the offerings of Leviticus, the peace offering carries a unique warmth. While other offerings speak of consecration or restoration, this one points toward fellowship. It is an offering that makes room for communion, a celebration that God has welcomed His people into His presence. At its heart is not merely an altar, but a table. The God who dwells among His people does not simply call them to obey Him; He desires to share life with them.

Throughout Scripture, tables become places where God's heart is revealed. Abraham welcomes mysterious guests beneath the oaks of Mamre. Israel eats the Passover in hope of freedom. David sings of a table prepared in the presence of his enemies. Again and again, meals become signs that God delights not merely in sustaining life but in sharing it. The peace offering belongs to this same story. It reminds us that holiness is not only found in solemn moments of worship but also in joyful fellowship with the One who invites us to draw near.

This thread reaches a beautiful fulfillment on the road to Emmaus. Two weary disciples walk home carrying shattered expectations. Though the risen Christ walks beside them, grief keeps them from recognizing Him. It is only when they invite Him to stay, and when He takes bread, blesses it, breaks it, and gives it to them, that their eyes are opened. They do not recognize Him first in a miracle or a sermon, but at a shared table. The place of ordinary hospitality becomes the place of extraordinary revelation.

There is something deeply comforting about this. We often expect God to meet us only in moments of great spiritual intensity—in mountain-top experiences, dramatic answers to prayer, or seasons of unmistakable certainty. Yet Christ so often reveals Himself in quieter places: over bread and conversation, in the welcome offered to a stranger, in laughter shared with friends, or in the stillness of an evening meal. The sacred is not far removed from the ordinary. It is hidden within it, waiting for attentive hearts.

Perhaps this is why peace is so much richer in Scripture than simply the absence of conflict. The peace of God is the wholeness that comes from restored relationship. It is the settled joy of knowing we have a place in His house. It is the freedom to stop striving for acceptance because we have already been welcomed. Like the disciples at Emmaus, we discover that our hearts begin to burn not because we have mastered every mystery, but because Love has chosen to sit down at the table with us.

Today, imagine Christ drawing up a chair beside you. He is present not only in your prayers, but in your meals, your conversations, your work, and your moments of quiet rest. He still takes ordinary bread and fills it with extraordinary grace. He still opens eyes that have grown dim with disappointment. And He still invites His friends to the table, where peace is no longer something we pursue but Someone with whom we share our lives. The journey into God's presence always leads, in the end, to communion.

> *Today I receive the peace of Christ and gladly take my place at His table.*
