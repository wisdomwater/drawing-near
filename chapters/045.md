## Day 45 — Everything Belongs to God

**Leviticus 27:30–33**

**James 1:17**

> *"The holiest hands are not those that cling to God's gifts, but those that receive them with gratitude and return them in love."*

The final chapter of Leviticus closes with an act of offering. A portion of the harvest, the fruit of the trees, and the increase of the flocks were set apart as holy to the Lord. At first glance, this may seem like a matter of obligation, yet beneath the practice lies a deeper invitation. The tithe was never meant to suggest that only a tenth belonged to God while the remainder belonged to us. Rather, the offering became a joyful confession that everything had always come from His generous hand. Returning a portion was a way of remembering the true Giver behind every gift.

This changes the way we see the world. It is easy to imagine ourselves as owners, carefully guarding what we have earned and accumulated. Yet Scripture gently invites us to become stewards instead. The earth produces its fruit, the rains fall in their season, the breath fills our lungs, and the strength to labor is itself a gift. Even the ordinary moments we often overlook—a shared meal, a friend's laughter, a sunrise, the quiet beating of our hearts—arrive as expressions of God's continual generosity. Gratitude begins wherever we awaken to the truth that life itself is grace.

James echoes this same vision with beautiful simplicity: *"Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above."* He points our attention beyond the gifts to the Father of lights, whose goodness does not flicker or fade. The gifts of life are beautiful, but they are never meant to become our ultimate treasure. They are rays of sunlight leading us back to the sun itself. Every kindness we receive whispers something about the heart of the One who delights to give. Every blessing becomes an invitation into deeper communion with the Giver.

When we begin to live this way, generosity ceases to feel like losing something. Open hands are no longer empty hands; they are hands prepared to receive again. We discover that love cannot flow through clenched fists. The more tightly we grasp what we have, the more anxious we become about protecting it. But when we remember that all things come from God and ultimately return to Him, fear begins to loosen its hold. We are free to share our time, our resources, our encouragement, and our compassion because we trust that the wellspring of God's goodness is never exhausted.

This spirit of offering reaches beyond money or possessions. We can offer our work, our conversations, our creativity, our sorrows, and even our ordinary routines. A meal prepared with love, a word spoken with gentleness, a patient response in a difficult moment—all become quiet offerings laid upon the altar of daily life. In Christ, the boundary between the sacred and the ordinary begins to disappear. The whole of life becomes a place where God's presence is welcomed, and every act of love becomes an expression of thanksgiving.

It is fitting that Leviticus ends here. The journey that began with sacrifices and the longing for God to dwell among His people concludes with the reminder that everything already belongs to Him. From the garden where all creation was received as gift, to the tabernacle filled with God's glory, to Christ who gave Himself completely, to the Church learning to live as a grateful people, and finally to the New Creation where every good thing finds its fulfillment in God's presence, Scripture tells one continuous story of divine generosity. We lose nothing by placing our lives in His hands. We simply discover that they have always been there.

> *Today I will receive every good gift with gratitude and offer my whole life back to God in love.*
