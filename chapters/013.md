## Day 13 — Glory Fills the House

**Leviticus 9:22–24**

**John 1:14**

> *"The glory of God is not distant brilliance, but Love choosing to make its home among us."*

After days of preparation, waiting, and consecration, the long-awaited moment finally arrived. Aaron lifted his hands in blessing, Moses and Aaron entered the Tent of Meeting together, and when they emerged, the glory of the Lord appeared before all the people. Fire came forth from God's presence and consumed the offering upon the altar. The people could only respond with joyful cries before falling to the ground in awe. The tabernacle had become what it was always intended to be: a dwelling place where heaven and earth met in holy communion.

The fire was not the climax because it was spectacular. It was the climax because it revealed that God had drawn near. Every sacrifice, every careful instruction, every thread woven into the tabernacle, every act of consecration had pointed toward this one desire: that the Holy One would dwell in the midst of His people. The glory of God is not merely overwhelming power. It is the radiant beauty of divine presence. Glory is what love looks like when it is fully revealed.

John reaches for this same language when he writes, "The Word became flesh and lived among us." The Greek word he uses carries the sense of pitching a tent, of tabernacling among humanity. What once filled the Tent of Meeting now shines through the life of Jesus. The glory that once appeared as fire upon an altar is now seen in a face full of grace and truth. God's deepest longing has never changed. He does not merely visit His creation from time to time. He comes to make His home with us, revealing Himself not through distance, but through nearness.

The glory of Christ often appears in ways we would never expect. It shines in mercy offered instead of condemnation. It appears in meals shared with those whom others overlooked. It is seen in tears shed beside a grieving family, in hands stretched out to heal, and in forgiveness spoken from a cross. We are inclined to search for glory in the extraordinary, yet Jesus teaches us to recognize it in humble love. Wherever self-giving love is present, the radiance of God is quietly breaking into the world.

This changes how we look at our own lives. We may imagine that God's presence belongs only to moments of intense worship or unusual spiritual experience. Yet the New Testament gently widens the horizon. Through the Spirit, our lives become living temples, places where divine glory longs to be revealed. Every home can become a sanctuary where kindness is practiced. Every table can become a place of blessing. Every conversation can become an opportunity for grace. We need not chase God's presence across great distances when He delights to dwell in hearts that welcome Him with trust.

The people of Israel responded to God's glory with joyful wonder and humble reverence. Both belong together. Awe is not fear of being driven away, but joy at discovering that the Creator desires communion with His creation. The journey that began in the garden, continued through the tabernacle, and shone perfectly in Christ now continues within His people. Day by day, His presence transforms ordinary lives into holy places. And as His love fills the house of our hearts, the truest glory is not that we have found God, but that God has graciously come to dwell with us.

> *Today I welcome the God whose glory is revealed in grace, and whose home is among His people.*
