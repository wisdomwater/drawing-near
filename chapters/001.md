## Day 1 — God Speaks from the Tent

**Leviticus 1:1**

**Exodus 40:34–38**

> *"The story of God begins not with our search for Him, but with His desire to dwell among us."*

Leviticus opens with a surprising image. Before there are sacrifices to offer, commandments to receive, or rituals to observe, there is a tent. It stands quietly in the center of the camp, wrapped in the radiant mystery of God's presence. The people do not summon Him there. They do not persuade Him to come. He has already chosen to dwell among them. The first movement of this book is not humanity reaching toward heaven, but heaven stooping toward humanity.

The final chapter of Exodus prepares us for this moment. The tabernacle has been completed, and a cloud settles upon it like a living veil. By day the people see the cloud. By night it burns with fire. Whenever the cloud moves, they move. Whenever it rests, they rest. Their lives are no longer governed merely by plans or ambitions, but by the quiet rhythm of God's abiding presence. The tabernacle becomes more than a place of worship; it becomes the heart from which an entire way of life flows.

Then, with remarkable simplicity, Leviticus begins: "Yahweh called to Moses." God speaks from within the Tent of Meeting. The first word of Leviticus is not a demand but an invitation. The One who filled the sanctuary now calls a human being into conversation. Every instruction that follows grows out of relationship. Before God teaches His people how to live, He assures them that He is with them.

This pattern echoes throughout the whole story of Scripture. In the Garden of Eden, God walked among the trees in the cool of the day, seeking fellowship with humanity. In the wilderness, He dwelt in a tent at the center of Israel's camp. In Jesus Christ, "the Word became flesh and dwelt among us." At Pentecost, the Spirit made ordinary men and women into living temples. The Bible ends with the same promise with which Leviticus begins: "Behold, God's dwelling is with humanity." From beginning to end, the deepest desire of God is not merely to instruct us, forgive us, or bless us. His deepest desire is to be with us.

This changes the way we think about the spiritual life. We often imagine that faith begins with our search for God, as though He were hidden in some distant place, waiting to be discovered by those who strive hard enough. Yet the witness of Scripture tells another story. God always makes the first move. Long before we whisper a prayer, He is already present. Long before we take a single step toward Him, He has drawn near to us. The journey of faith is not persuading God to come closer; it is awakening to the One who has never ceased to dwell in our midst.

Today, imagine your own life as a tent in the wilderness. There are places of joy and places of uncertainty. There are unfinished corners and well-worn paths. Yet somewhere at the center, often hidden beneath the noise of daily life, God waits—not impatiently, but faithfully. He does not shout from a distance. He speaks from within the place where He has chosen to dwell. Perhaps the invitation of this first day is simply to become still enough to hear His voice again.

> *Today I awaken to the God who has already drawn near.*
