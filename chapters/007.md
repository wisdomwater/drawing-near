## Day 7 — Life Belongs to God

**Leviticus 3:16–17**

**John 10:10**

> *"The life we long to possess most deeply is the life we are first invited to receive."*

As the peace offering draws to a close, Leviticus reminds Israel that certain things are not theirs to claim. "All the fat is Yahweh's," and the blood, which throughout Scripture symbolizes life itself, is not to be consumed. At first, these instructions may seem like simple restrictions. Yet beneath them is a beautiful confession: life is sacred because it belongs to God. The deepest source of life cannot be owned, controlled, or manufactured. It can only be received as a gift.

This is one of the quiet lessons woven throughout the wilderness journey. Every sunrise, every breath, every drop of water from the rock, every measure of manna upon the ground reminded Israel that existence itself flowed from the generous hand of God. The laws surrounding blood became a continual invitation to remember that life is never merely a possession. It is participation in the divine generosity that sustains all things. To honor life was to honor its Giver.

When Jesus declares, "I came that they may have life, and may have it abundantly," He gathers this ancient hope into Himself. He does not simply promise longer days or easier circumstances. He offers a life rooted in communion with the Father—a life that cannot be measured by wealth, success, or comfort. It is the life of a branch abiding in the vine, of living water rising from within, of bread that satisfies the deepest hunger of the soul. Abundant life is not found by grasping more tightly, but by remaining close to the Source from whom all life flows.

How easily we forget this. We often live as though life depends entirely upon our own strength, our planning, or our ability to hold everything together. We clutch at time, accomplishments, relationships, even our own identities, afraid that letting go will leave us with less. Yet the gospel gently teaches another way. What belongs to God is safest in God's hands. The One who gives life is far more capable of sustaining it than we are.

This changes how we see the ordinary moments of our days. A shared meal becomes more than nourishment; it becomes thanksgiving. A quiet walk beneath the trees becomes an opportunity to notice the breath that fills our lungs without our asking. Even seasons of weakness remind us that we are upheld by grace rather than by our own endless striving. The more we recognize life as gift, the more gratitude begins to replace anxiety, and trust slowly loosens the grip of fear.

The command that "life belongs to God" is therefore not a barrier but a blessing. It frees us from the exhausting illusion that everything depends upon us. We are invited instead to live with open hands, receiving each day as fresh mercy. Christ has come not merely to improve our lives, but to share His own life with us. As we dwell in Him, we discover that abundance is not measured by how much we accumulate, but by how deeply we participate in the inexhaustible love of God.

> *Today I receive my life as a gift and rest in the One from whom all life flows.*
