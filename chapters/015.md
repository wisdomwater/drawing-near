## Day 15 — Be Holy

**Leviticus 11:44–45**

**1 Peter 1:15–16**

> *"Holiness is not becoming less human, but becoming fully alive in the love of God."*

At first hearing, the call to "be holy, for I am holy" can sound impossibly distant, as though God were asking us to become something beyond our reach. Yet Leviticus anchors this invitation not in human effort, but in divine relationship. Before God calls His people to holiness, He reminds them, "I am Yahweh your God... who brought you up out of the land of Egypt." Holiness begins not with achievement but with deliverance. The One who calls them is already the One who has drawn near, set them free, and chosen to dwell among them.

To be holy is, in its deepest sense, to belong. A treasured cup is set apart not because it despises ordinary dishes, but because it is reserved for a particular purpose. So too, God's people are invited to become a community whose lives reflect the beauty of the One they love. Holiness is not withdrawal from the world but wholehearted participation in God's life within the world. It is the slow shaping of the heart until love, mercy, truth, and compassion become as natural as breathing.

Peter hears these ancient words and discovers that they still speak to the followers of Christ. The call has not changed, though it now shines with greater clarity in Jesus. If we wonder what God's holiness looks like in human life, we need only look to Christ. Holiness washes weary feet. It touches those others avoid. It welcomes children, eats with sinners, forgives enemies, and remains faithful even through suffering. In Jesus, holiness is no longer an abstract idea. It has a face, a voice, and hands stretched wide in self-giving love.

Perhaps we have sometimes mistaken holiness for perfectionism. We imagine that every flaw disqualifies us or that God waits impatiently for us to become flawless before drawing near. But the story of Scripture tells another tale. Again and again, God walks with imperfect people, patiently forming them over years rather than moments. Holiness is less like polished marble and more like a garden. It grows quietly through daily light, faithful tending, and the hidden work of living roots. Grace does not rush this process because love never forces what it longs to cultivate.

This means holiness can be practiced in the smallest places. It is found in choosing honesty when deception would be easier, extending forgiveness when resentment feels justified, speaking gently when irritation rises, and making room for someone who has been overlooked. Every ordinary act of love becomes another thread woven into the fabric of a holy life. We need not search for extraordinary opportunities to become more like Christ. Today's conversation, today's work, today's interruptions, and today's moments of quiet all become fertile ground where His likeness can grow.

The God who called Israel out of Egypt still calls people into freedom today—not merely freedom from sin, but freedom for love. His holiness is not a wall that keeps us away but a light that draws us home. As we remain near Christ, the Holy One gently reshapes our desires until His life begins to blossom within our own. The command, "Be holy," is therefore not a burden laid upon weary shoulders. It is an invitation to become who we were created to be: women and men in whom the beauty of God's own love quietly shines.

> *Today I rest in the Holy One, trusting His love to shape my life into His likeness.*
