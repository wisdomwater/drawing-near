## Day 22 — Leave Room for the Poor

**Leviticus 19:9–10**

**Ruth 2:1–10**

> *"Love always leaves room at the edges for someone else to be welcomed in."*

When Israel gathered in the harvest, God instructed them not to strip the fields bare. The corners were to remain untouched, and the scattered grain was not to be collected. What appears at first to be an agricultural instruction is, at its heart, a lesson in abundance. The land belonged to God, and because it was His gift, its fruit was never meant to be consumed without regard for others. Even the fields were to bear witness that God's generosity always extends beyond the one who first receives His blessings.

There is something deeply beautiful about this image. God builds generosity into the rhythm of ordinary life. He does not wait for extraordinary moments of charity. Instead, He teaches His people to live in such a way that provision naturally overflows toward those in need. The edges of the field become places of hope, where the poor and the foreigner may gather enough to live with dignity. God's kingdom is not marked by grasping hands that gather every last grain, but by open hands that trust there will always be enough because the Giver remains faithful.

The story of Ruth allows us to walk into one of those fields. A widow from a foreign land, carrying little more than grief and uncertainty, arrives where God's command has already prepared a place for her. Boaz does more than obey the letter of the law; he embodies its spirit. He welcomes Ruth with kindness, protects her from harm, and quietly enlarges the generosity already woven into God's command. What began as permission to glean becomes an encounter with unexpected grace. Long before Ruth realizes it, God has been arranging a homecoming through the ordinary faithfulness of His people.

Jesus reveals this same abundance wherever He goes. His table always seems to have another place. His compassion is never exhausted by the number of those who come seeking healing. He feeds the hungry, welcomes the overlooked, and continually notices those standing at the edges of society. In Christ we see that the heart of God is never merely to meet a minimum obligation but to create a community where no one is invisible. The kingdom grows wherever people discover that there is room for them after all.

Our own fields may not be planted with barley or vineyards, but each of us has edges. We have time that could become an unhurried conversation. We have resources that can become quiet generosity. We have tables with an empty chair, attention that can be freely given, words that encourage instead of dismiss, and patience that allows another person to feel seen. It is easy to fill every corner of our lives until nothing remains but exhaustion and self-preservation. Yet God's wisdom gently asks whether we might leave a little space—space where grace can enter through the needs of another. Often it is in those unplanned interruptions that Christ Himself meets us.

From Eden's overflowing garden to the fields of Bethlehem, from the table of Christ to the feast of the new creation, God has always been making room. His desire has never been to gather everything into His own hands, but to share His life freely with the world He loves. As we learn to leave room at the edges of our own lives, we begin to reflect the spaciousness of His heart. And we may discover, as Ruth did, that the places where we extend mercy become the very places where God's quiet redemption unfolds.

> *Today I will leave room for grace, trusting that God's abundance is always enough for us all.*
