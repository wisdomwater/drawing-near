## Day 2 — Drawing Near

**Leviticus 1:2–4**

**James 4:8**

> *"Every step toward God is already met by the One who has been waiting with open arms."*

The first offering described in Leviticus begins with a simple act: someone comes near. Before there is fire upon the altar or smoke rising toward heaven, there are footsteps across the camp toward the Tent of Meeting. An ordinary man or woman leaves the familiar rhythms of daily life and walks toward the place where God has chosen to dwell. The offering matters, but so does the journey. Every sacrifice begins with the quiet decision to draw near.

The worshiper places a hand upon the head of the offering before it is given. It is a gesture of identification, of trust, of bringing one's whole life before God. Nothing is hidden. Joy and sorrow, gratitude and failure, hope and fear all come together beneath that gentle touch. The altar is not merely a place where something is surrendered; it is a place where the worshiper learns that they themselves are welcomed. Long before anything is consumed by the fire, the person is received by the God who invited them there.

James echoes this same invitation centuries later: "Draw near to God, and he will draw near to you." These words can sound like a condition, as though God waits at a distance until we have taken enough faithful steps. Yet the whole story of Scripture tells us otherwise. God has already come near—in the garden, in the tabernacle, in Christ, and through the Spirit who now dwells within His people. Our drawing near is not persuading God to become present. It is awakening to the Presence that has never abandoned us.

This is often how the spiritual life unfolds. We imagine that we must arrive before we are welcomed, become worthy before we are embraced, or finally quiet our restless hearts before God will receive us. But the Tent of Meeting stands in the middle of the camp before anyone approaches it. The invitation exists before the response. Even our desire to seek God is itself a quiet stirring of His grace already at work within us.

Perhaps drawing near today looks less dramatic than you expected. It may be five unhurried minutes of silence before the day begins. It may be a whispered prayer while washing dishes, a deep breath taken during a difficult conversation, or the choice to notice the beauty of sunlight spilling across the kitchen table. Every small turning of the heart becomes another step toward the Tent. We discover that God's presence is not confined to sacred places, because through Christ the true sanctuary has begun to fill every ordinary moment with holy possibility.

The invitation of Leviticus is not simply to bring something to God, but to bring yourself. Come with your questions that have no easy answers. Come with the wounds you still carry and the gratitude you have not yet spoken aloud. Come with empty hands if that is all you have today. The God who called from the Tent still delights to meet those who simply come near, and every sincere step toward Him becomes a quiet discovery that He has been walking toward us all along.

> *Today I rest in the God who gladly welcomes all who draw near.*
